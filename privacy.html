Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2, 2026

OpenBWC is an open-source research project focused on the ethical and transparent analysis of body-worn camera footage. Privacy is core to what we build, and it is core to how we run this website. This policy explains what information we collect when you visit openbwc.org and how we use it.

The short version

  • We do not sell your data, and we do not run advertising trackers.
  • We use privacy-friendly analytics that do not identify you personally.
  • We only use information you send us (for example, a contact message) to reply to you.

Analytics

We use TelemetryDeck to understand how the site is used (for example, which pages are viewed most). TelemetryDeck is designed to be privacy-first: it does not use cookies and does not collect data that identifies you as an individual. We use these aggregate insights only to improve the website.

Information you choose to send us

If you contact us through the form on our Contact page or by email, we receive the information you provide (such as your name, email address, and message). We use it solely to respond to you and to keep a record of the conversation. We do not share it with third parties for marketing.

Body-worn camera footage

This website does not collect, upload, or process any body-worn camera footage. Our research tools are built to run offline and to keep sensitive data under the control of the organizations that own it. Nothing you read about our analysis work involves data being collected through this site.

Third-party links

Our site links to external sites such as GitHub, LinkedIn, and our partners. Those services have their own privacy policies, and we are not responsible for their practices.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

Contact

If you have any questions about this policy, please reach out through our Contact page.
